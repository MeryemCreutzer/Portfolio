Internal Knowledge Management Optimization
Project

Overview

This initiative focused on turning a disorganized and underused SharePoint into a centralized, functional, and dynamic knowledge hub while also bridging gaps in internal documentation by identifying, creating, and enriching essential content across the Product Documentation department.

📌 Key outcomes:

  • Centralization of scattered internal documentation.
  • Streamlining access to process checklists via Microsoft Lists.
  • Collecting feedback through Microsoft Forms to improve documentation and site usability.
  • Better visibility and usability of Docs and Elearning/Training work and management documents.
  • Contributions to achieving ISO 9001 certification.

Context & Background

When I joined the company, SharePoint was technically available but functionally dormant. Documentation was fragmented: random links, outdated files, and no clear structure. Essential processes lived in isolated Excel files or siloed folders, and knowledge sharing was minimal. This posed a significant risk to documentation quality, team efficiency, and audit readiness.

My Role

As the Technical Content Developer, I took ownership of this transformation. I conducted audits, mapped out pain points, and spearheaded the reorganization and optimization of our SharePoint space. I also led the implementation of tools like Microsoft Lists and Forms to replace static Excel-based workflows.

Challenges & Solutions

Process & Methodology

Audit & Mapping

  • Conducted a full audit of existing documentation and content sources. Identified scattered, redundant, and outdated files, missing process documentation, and inconsistent file naming and storage practices.

Architecture Design

  • Designed an intuitive SharePoint structure based on thematic areas (e.g., onboarding, process management, review checklists) to simplify access and navigation. Included quick links to Figma, Microsoft Lists, Forms, and related internal sites (marketing, support, product etc.).

Content Reclassification & Rebranding

  • Classified all documents by type, confidentiality, and status ensuring each document adheres to security protocols. Introduced a visual labeling system for better visibility and lifecycle tracking.

Modernization of Document Design & Workflow Management

  • Adopted Figma as a central platform for creating and managing workflow diagrams, style guides, and UI-related documents. This ensured brand consistency, collaboration, and real-time updates.
  • Integrated Microsoft Lists to replace outdated Excel checklists with dynamic, trackable entries. Used Microsoft Forms for continuous feedback collection and engagement across teams.

Final Outcome

The optimized SharePoint space is now a robust knowledge base that supports collaboration, training, audit readiness, and ongoing content quality. It played a role in our company obtaining ISO 9001 certification by ensuring content was accessible, structured, and maintained in alignment with process excellence standards.
